# Bengaluru Travel Guide

Welcome to Bengaluru (formerly Bangalore), the IT Hub and Garden City of India! Known for its beautiful parks, pleasant climate, and vibrant cafe and craft beer culture.

## Transportation Tips
- **Traffic Warning**: Bengaluru is infamous for its road traffic congestion. Plan your travel with extra buffer time, especially during office hours (9:00 AM to 11:00 AM and 6:00 PM to 8:30 PM).
- **Namma Metro**: An excellent, air-conditioned way to bypass road traffic. The Purple Line connects key eastern and western areas, and the Green Line covers north to south.
- **Airport Travel**: The Kempegowda International Airport is located about 40 km outside the main city. Use the official **KIA Vayu Vajra** AC buses operated by BMTC—they are comfortable, affordable, and run 24/7.
- **Ride-hailing**: Use Ola, Uber, or Namma Yatri for auto-rickshaws and cabs.

## Local Food Highlights
- **South Indian Breakfast**: You must try the *Masala Dosa* at legendary spots like Vidyarthi Bhavan in Gandhi Bazaar, CTR (Shree Sagar) in Malleshwaram, or MTR near Lalbagh. Pair it with aromatic *Filter Coffee*.
- **Microbreweries**: Bengaluru is the microbrewery capital of India. Areas like Indiranagar, Koramangala, and Whitefield are filled with places serving excellent craft beer and global cuisine (e.g., Toit, Windmills Craftworks, Arbor Brewing Company).
- **Cafe Culture**: Indiranagar and Koramangala host hundreds of trendy cafes serving specialty coffee and organic foods.

## Weather Guidelines
- **Pleasant Climate**: Bengaluru enjoys moderate, pleasant weather throughout the year. 
- **Rainy Season (June to October)**: The city receives moderate to heavy rain, often in the late afternoons or evenings. Always carry a folding umbrella.
- **Winter (November to January)**: Cool and breezy (temperatures between 15°C to 25°C). You might want a light sweater or jacket for early mornings and late evenings.

## Etiquette & Safety
- **Green Spaces**: Visit Cubbon Park or Lalbagh Botanical Garden in the early morning for peaceful walks. Note that vehicle entry is restricted in Cubbon Park on Sundays.
- **Language**: English and Hindi are widely understood, but learning a few words of Kannada (the local language) like *"Swalpa adjust madi"* (please adjust a bit) or *"Namskara"* (hello) is highly appreciated.
- **IT Culture**: The city is very cosmopolitan, safe, and has a large population of young working professionals.
