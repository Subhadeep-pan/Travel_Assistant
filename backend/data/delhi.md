# Delhi Travel Guide

Welcome to Delhi, the historic capital of India! This guide contains practical tips, local secrets, and cultural etiquette to help you navigate the city.

## Transportation Tips
- **Delhi Metro**: The Metro is the cleanest, fastest, and most reliable way to travel around Delhi. Get a **Smart Card** at any station to save time and avoid long ticket queues.
- **Auto-rickshaws**: Always negotiate the fare before getting in, or ask them to run on the meter (though many refuse). Alternatively, use ride-hailing apps like Uber or Ola to book auto-rickshaws with fixed pricing.
- **Peak Hours**: Avoid traveling between 8:30 AM to 10:30 AM and 5:30 PM to 8:00 PM, as the metro and roads are extremely crowded.

## Local Food Highlights
- **Street Food**: Try the famous *Chole Bhature* in Karol Bagh, *Golgappas* (water-filled crispy spheres) in Rajouri Garden, and *Aloo Chaat* in Connaught Place.
- **Old Delhi Feast**: Visit Chandni Chowk for legendary street food. Don't miss *Paranthe Wali Gali* for deep-fried Indian flatbreads, and eat traditional sweets like *Jalebis* at the Old Famous Jalebi Wala.
- **Fine Dining**: For upscale Indian food, head to Pandara Road or Connaught Place (CP).

## Weather Guidelines
- **Summer (April to June)**: Extremely hot (temperatures can exceed 45°C or 113°F). Stay hydrated, wear light cotton clothing, and schedule sightseeing for early morning or late evening.
- **Monsoon (July to September)**: Heavy rains can cause waterlogging on roads. Always carry an umbrella or raincoat.
- **Winter (November to February)**: Temperatures can drop to 5°C (41°F). Heavy fog is common. Pack warm sweaters, jackets, and thermal wear.

## Etiquette & Safety
- **Dress Code**: Dress modestly, especially when visiting places of worship like temples, mosques, and gurudwaras. Cover your shoulders and knees. Remove your shoes before entering any religious place.
- **Scams**: Be wary of touts at railway stations and around Connaught Place claiming that certain places are closed or offering "official" tourist office tours. Always verify information independently.
- **Tipping**: A 10% tip is standard in sit-down restaurants. If a service charge is already included in the bill, you do not need to tip extra.
