# Jaipur Travel Guide

Welcome to Jaipur, the Pink City and capital of Rajasthan! Part of India's Golden Triangle tourist circuit, Jaipur is famous for its stunning palaces, massive forts, and colorful traditional markets.

## Transportation Tips
- **E-rickshaws**: Battery-operated rickshaws are cheap and perfect for navigating the narrow lanes of the old walled city. Always negotiate and agree on the fare beforehand.
- **Autos and Cabs**: Uber and Ola operate in Jaipur and are recommended for longer distances, such as traveling to Amber Fort (located 11 km outside the city center).
- **Heritage Walk**: The old city (inside the pink walls) is best explored on foot. Start from Hawa Mahal and walk towards the bazaars.

## Local Food Highlights
- **Dal Baati Churma**: The signature Rajasthani dish. Hard wheat rolls (Baati) baked over charcoal, crushed and soaked in pure ghee, served with spicy lentil soup (Dal) and sweet crumbled wheat (Churma). Try it at Chokhi Dhani or LMB (Laxmi Misthan Bhandar).
- **Pyaaz Kachori**: Deep-fried pastry filled with spiced onions. Enjoy it hot in the morning at Rawat Misthan Bhandar.
- **Lassiwala**: Visit the original Lassiwala on MI Road (established in 1944) for thick, creamy sweet curd lassi served in traditional earthen clay cups called *kulhads*.
- **Traditional Sweets**: Try *Ghewar* (a disc-shaped sweet cake made from flour and sugar syrup) during the festival season.

## Weather Guidelines
- **Desert Climate**: Jaipur has extreme weather.
- **Summer (April to July)**: Scorching hot, with temperatures reaching 45°C (113°F). Carry sunscreen, hats, and drink lots of water or *shanch* (buttermilk) to stay hydrated. Avoid outdoor walking during midday.
- **Winter (November to February)**: Very pleasant sunny days (20°C) but nights can be cold, dropping to 5°C (41°F). Pack sweaters and jackets.

## Etiquette & Safety
- **Shopping**: Jaipur's bazaars (Johari Bazar, Bapu Bazar) are famous for jewelry, textiles, and handicrafts. Bargaining is expected—start by offering 40% to 50% of the quoted price and negotiate from there.
- **Guides**: Be cautious of tour guides at Amber Fort or City Palace who try to take you to specific handicraft shops—they receive large commissions, which inflates the price. Respectfully decline and stick to your sightseeing.
- **Dress Code**: Dress conservatively when visiting historical and religious sites.
