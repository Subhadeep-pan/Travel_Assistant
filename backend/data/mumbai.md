# Mumbai Travel Guide

Welcome to Mumbai (formerly Bombay), the financial capital of India and the home of Bollywood! It is a city that never sleeps.

## Transportation Tips
- **Mumbai Local Trains**: The lifeline of the city. Extremely fast and cheap, but incredibly crowded during rush hours (8:00 AM to 11:00 AM and 5:00 PM to 8:30 PM). If you ride, buy a first-class ticket or travel during mid-day (12:00 PM to 4:00 PM).
- **Kaali-Peeli Taxis & Autos**: Black-and-yellow taxis operate in South Mumbai (autos are not allowed here). Auto-rickshaws operate in the suburban areas (North of Bandra). They strictly run on meters.
- **Bridges**: Use the *Bandra-Worli Sea Link* to skip traffic between the suburbs and South Mumbai. It offers a spectacular drive.

## Local Food Highlights
- **Vada Pav**: The ultimate Mumbai street food. A spicy potato dumpling inside a bread bun, served with red and green chutneys. Best enjoyed hot from local street stalls.
- **Pav Bhaji**: Spiced vegetable mash cooked on a flat griddle and served with buttered bread rolls. Try it at Sardar Pav Bhaji or Cannon Pav Bhaji near CST.
- **Chaat at Chowpatty**: Visit Girgaon Chowpatty beach in the evening to try *Bhel Puri* (puffed rice mix) and *Sev Puri*.
- **Coastal Cuisine**: Try fresh seafood like Bombay Duck or Surmai fry at Mahesh Lunch Home or Trishna.

## Weather Guidelines
- **Monsoon (June to September)**: Mumbai receives extremely heavy rainfall. Local trains can occasionally halt due to flooded tracks. High tides during monsoons near Marine Drive are beautiful but can be dangerous; stay clear of the edge.
- **Summer & Humid months**: Hot and humid all year round, except from December to February. Carry wet wipes, stay hydrated, and wear light clothes.
- **Winter (December to February)**: Mildly pleasant with cool evenings (18°C to 25°C). Excellent time for walking tours around the Fort heritage district.

## Etiquette & Safety
- **Fast-Paced**: People in Mumbai are always in a hurry but are generally very helpful. Use the phrase *"Excuse me"* or *"Bhaiya"* (brother) to get attention in crowds.
- **Safety**: Mumbai is considered one of the safest cities in India, even for solo female travelers at night. Standard precautions still apply.
- **Gateway of India**: If visiting the Gateway, book ferry tickets to Elephanta Caves early in the morning to avoid long queues and midday heat.
