# Kolkata Travel Guide

Welcome to Kolkata (formerly Calcutta), the City of Joy! Kolkata is famous for its grand colonial architecture, rich artistic heritage, and legendary food.

## Transportation Tips
- **Yellow Taxis**: The iconic yellow ambassador cabs run on meters, but drivers often quote fixed rates. Agree on the fare before starting or use Uber/Yatri Sathi apps.
- **Kolkata Metro**: The oldest metro network in India. It is highly efficient. Recently, the East-West line introduced underwater metro travel under the Hooghly River.
- **Trams**: Kolkata is the only Indian city with an active tram network. Trams are slow but offer a wonderful, nostalgic journey through old parts of the city.
- **Hand-pulled Rickshaws**: Still present in areas like Bara Bazar. Try to walk or use other transport if you feel uncomfortable using them.

## Local Food Highlights
- **Sweets**: Kolkata is world-famous for its sweets. You must try *Rosogolla* (spongy cheese balls in syrup), *Sandesh*, and *Mishti Doi* (sweet baked yogurt). Famous sweet shops include KC Das, Balaram Mullick, and Bhim Chandra Nag.
- **Street Food**: Try *Phuchka* (Kolkata's version of panipuri, filled with spicy potato mashed with tamarind water) at Vardaan Market, and *Kathi Rolls* (paratha wrap filled with spiced paneer or vegetables) at Kusum Rolls in Park Street.
- **Traditional Bengali Cuisine**: Visit Bhojohori Manna or 6 Ballygunge Place to try *Luchi* (puffed bread) with *Cholar Dal*, and fish specialties like *Sorse Ilish* (mustard fish).

## Weather Guidelines
- **Summer (March to June)**: Hot and extremely humid. Temperatures hover around 35°C to 40°C, but the humidity makes it feel much warmer. Wear loose, light clothing.
- **Monsoon (June to September)**: Heavy downpours are common, often leading to street flooding. Carry an umbrella at all times.
- **Winter (November to February)**: Very pleasant and cool (12°C to 20°C). Perfect for walking tours. A light jacket or shawl is sufficient.

## Etiquette & Safety
- **Culture**: Bengali people are passionate about literature, arts, and football. Engaging in friendly debates about sports or books is a great way to connect.
- **Dress Code**: Modest dressing is recommended. When visiting temples (like Kalighat or Dakshineswar), wear clothes that cover shoulders and knees.
- **Pace of Life**: The city has a slower, more laid-back pace than Mumbai or Delhi. Shops and offices might open a bit later (around 10:00 AM or 11:00 AM).
